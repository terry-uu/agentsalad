# Philosophy

## The Problem

AI agents are powerful. But the barrier to using them is unreasonably high.

Jargon-heavy documentation, complex configurations, developer-only toolchains — if you're not already technical, you simply cannot access what agents have to offer. The result is predictable: agents get wrapped into SaaS products, middlemen multiply, and the people who would benefit most end up paying the most.

The gap between those who build and those who use keeps widening.

## What We Believe

A spare computer at home is all it takes.

Anyone should be able to run their own AI agent — not just developers, not just companies with infrastructure teams. The technology is already here. What's missing is the bridge between "possible" and "accessible."

We believe the benefits of AI should raise everyone's floor, not just raise the ceiling for a few.

## What Agent Salad Does

Agent Salad strips away the complexity:

1. **Create an Agent** — pick a provider, choose a model, describe what it should do
2. **Connect a Channel** — paste a bot token
3. **Add a Target** — who should it talk to

That's it. Three steps. A visual UI. No CLI, no config files, no deployment pipelines.

One Node.js process on a machine you already own. Your agent, your rules, your data.

## Why Elastic License 2.0

We chose the Elastic License because it protects the intent behind this project.

- **Use it freely** — personal, internal, commercial, anything
- **Modify it, fork it, learn from it** — the source is open for a reason
- **But don't turn it into a managed service** — don't take this project and rebuild the exact middleman structure it was designed to eliminate

If someone packages Agent Salad into a SaaS product and charges for access, they're recreating the gap we set out to close. The license exists to prevent that.

## The Goal

We want a world where running your own AI agent is as normal as running your own Wi-Fi router. Not a luxury. Not a service you subscribe to. Just something you set up once and it works for you.

The technology should serve everyone — not just those who can afford the subscription.
